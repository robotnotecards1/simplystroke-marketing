The rundown

Playing Torrey Pines South

Torrey Pines South is a city-owned muni, which still catches people off guard. You can play the same holes the pros play at the Farmers Insurance Open, on the same cliffs that hosted the 2008 and 2021 U.S. Opens, for a tee time and a booking window instead of a membership and an invitation. That is the whole appeal, and it is a real one.

It is also hard. Rees Jones stretched the South past 7,600 yards for the 2008 Open, and the wind off the Pacific handles the rest. The greens get firm by early afternoon, the rough is thick enough to cost you a shot, and the closing holes along the ocean take strokes you did not plan to give away. Most first-timers lose the round on club selection — the ocean makes everything look downhill when half of it is not. Bring more club than your eyes are telling you.

Here is the part you will not find on the course's own website: across the 312 rounds SimplyStroke golfers have posted here, the South plays about 17 shots over par for a mid-handicap. The hole that does the most damage is not the famous cliffside 4th — it is the par-4 12th, playing nearly a full stroke over par across every round logged.

Common questions

Torrey Pines South FAQ

What does the average golfer shoot at Torrey Pines South?

Across 312 rounds posted by SimplyStroke golfers, the average 18-hole score is 89.4, about 17 over par. Roughly a quarter of rounds land in the 85–89 range, and only about 4% break 75.

Is Torrey Pines South walkable?

It's a hilly walk with real elevation between several holes, but walking is allowed and caddies are available. Most casual players take a cart; purists walk it for the full experience.

How much does it cost to play Torrey Pines South?

Rates are tiered: San Diego residents with a card pay a reduced fee, while non-residents pay a premium weekend rate. Check the course site for current pricing, as munis adjust seasonally.

Do you need a handicap to play?

No handicap is required to book a public tee time. It's a demanding course, so newer players should expect a long day, but anyone can play it.
