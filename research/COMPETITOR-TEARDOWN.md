## Complaint #1 — Subscription fatigue. This is the loudest pain in the corpus, by a distance.

The highest-upvoted competitor comment in the entire dataset is someone leaving over price:

> **+493** — *"They're pretty good, but when I saw the price for a full year subscription, I just replaced the whole thing with shot scope instead."*

It keeps going, and the upvote numbers are unusually high — this is the thing r/golf agrees on:

> **+160** — *"I had arccos for 3 years but now at the $200 annual cost I cancelled."*
> **+120** — *"I loved Arccos as a system and the data I got, but their subscription price is ridiculously expensive and it keeps going up."*
> **+120** — *"For a one time $150 price I now use Shot Scope, get most of the same data and it's not a subscription based service to get it."*
> **+107** — *"You think I'm just going to pocket that money from cancelling the Arccos subscription?"*
> **+84** — *"No subscription is why I went with a ShotScope watch and trackers."*
> **+70** — *"The subscription is exactly why I went with ShotScope."*
> **+24** — *"I use a garmin watch and no extra subscriptions."*

And on GHIN, the one nobody can escape:

> *"any other app as crap as the GHIN app requiring a $50 ANNUAL subscription would be dead in the water if it wasn't for the USGA making it the only 'official' handicap app"*

**Market against:** price, and specifically *recurring* price. "No subscription" is not a feature in this market — it is **the** feature. Golfers are choosing demonstrably worse hardware and software purely to avoid a recurring charge. If SimplyStroke is one-time or free, that belongs in the headline, not the pricing page.

## Complaint #2 — Bloat. 18Birdies is the soft target.

> **+19** — *"18 birdies is a bloated mess these days."*
> **+23** — *"Have both and hate 18 birdies compare to the Grint"*
> *"I tried 18birdies (too expensive, too much going on), Hole19 and Golfshot."*

**Market against:** 18Birdies by name. It was the category default and it's now the category punchline. "Too much going on" is a direct invitation for a product called *Simply* Stroke.

---

## Complaint #3 — Friction during the round. This is your actual product wedge.

This is the most product-relevant complaint in the corpus and almost nobody is solving it:

> **+13** — *"I use 18 birdies, but I hate that I can't just tell it I missed the green, it demands to know if I was long, short, left, right, or didn't have a chance plus what club I used, etc."*

That is a man describing, precisely, why he will eventually stop using the app. He wants to record a number. It wants a dataset.

Related — Arccos can't handle the shot that actually matters:

> **+136** — *"As an arccos user, one of my main frustrations is, that I cannot log where I hit a ball that went oob/lost/in the water."*

And the post-round tax:

> *"It's really annoying, I end up have[ing] to use pen/paper and entering on GHIN after."*
> *"Shotscope: most complete shot tracking software BUT hardware is slow/display sucks and reconciling rounds is a chore"*

**Market against:** taps-per-hole. Every competitor optimises for *data richness*; every golfer complains about *data entry*. The winning claim is a number: "Score a hole in one tap." If SimplyStroke can't say something like that, the wedge isn't sharp enough yet.

## Complaint #4 — Battery

> *"The Grint sucked my watch battery dry."*
> *"I like Golfshot for feature richness... but it does drain the battery."*
> *"Battery life on the watch is down to about 50% after a day w regular wear..."* (Hole19)
> **+79** — *"I wouldn't get a dedicated watch but I'd get a Garmin just because of battery life."*

**Market against:** if there's a watch or phone story, lightweight = long battery. Garmin's entire advantage here is that it doesn't die.

---

## Complaint #5 — The phone itself

> **+26** — *"I don't bring my phone on the golf course."*

Not a competitor complaint — a *category* complaint, and the thing that limits everyone including you. Worth designing around explicitly.

---

## What competitors are praised for (know what you're up against)

- **Free tier that actually works:** *"18 birdies keeps it all in line and it's free"* (+123); *"18birdies without the premium subscription gives you the distance to the green"* (+109). 18Birdies' free tier is genuinely well-liked — beating them on "free" alone won't work. Beat them on *simple*.
- **One-time purchase:** Shot Scope's whole reputation.
- **Battery:** Garmin's whole reputation.
- **Grandfathered pricing:** TheGrint's loyalty mechanism — *"I've just paid $20/yr for Grint since I got grandfathered in."*

## The positioning this hands you

Golfers have told you, in their own words and with upvotes attached, exactly what they want:

**No subscription. Not bloated. Doesn't demand data you don't want to give. Doesn't kill your battery. Doesn't make you get your phone out.**

That is a product brief, and it's a better one than the ADHD angle — because *these* people are already angry, already switching, and already telling each other where to go. Right now they're going to Shot Scope, and paying $150 for hardware, because nothing simpler exists.
